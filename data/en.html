There’s significant misinformation about the explosions, fires, and violence at the Boston Marathon, JFK Library, MIT, and Watertown the week of Monday, 15 April 2013. We’ve been making a concise, live-time, factual summary of the situation and how people can help and get help.

Getting Around

MBTA

All subway service has resumed except for Copley Square, which remains closed. MBTA suggests using the Arlington or Back Bay stations instead. (3:20PM, 20 Apr)

Bus service is largely uninterrupted, with issues around Copley Square. For details, see the MBTA site. (3:20PM, 19 Apr)

All commuter rail service is back. (3:20PM, 19 Apr)

Mass Ave Bridge

Open in both directions, but travel is discouraged (8:42PM, 15 Apr).

Getting Help

You can use this page from the Red Cross to find help near you. The Red Cross has a relief center set up in Cambridge, MA (3:08PM, 15 Apr).

The Salvation Army has relief services on the scene downtown (15 Apr).

The Castle at Park Plaza, at the intersection of Columbus Avenue and Arlington Street, opened as a resource and information center offering counseling and shelter services. It has re-opened as of 9 a.m. Tuesday (16 Apr).

If you need help talking to your kids, this blog post by the Boston Public Health blog offers some tips to broaching the subject of tragedy.

For other tips or options, see #BostonHelp.

If you are a business owner located within the crime scene, you can retrieve your mail at your local post office with an ID (3:42PM, 17 Apr)

The City of Boston opened a Resource and Information Center in the lobby of the Boston Park Plaza Hotel for Back Bay area businesses impacted by yesterday’s tragedy. This satellite office will will be open from 9:00 a.m. – 6:00 p.m. Tuesday through Thursday. City officials from the Inspectional Services Department, Boston Redevelopment Authority, and Department of Neighborhood Development will be on hand to issue building permits, respond to questions about reopening and required inspections, and other additional business services(16 Apr).

Boston area taxpayers, and other affected by Monday’s explosions, have a three-month tax filing and payment extension; the IRS has said in a statement released Tuesday, 4/16 (7:39PM 16 Apr). This relief applies to all individual taxpayers who live in Suffolk County, Mass., including the city of Boston. It also includes victims, their families, first responders, others impacted by this tragedy who live outside Suffolk County and taxpayers whose tax preparers were adversely affected. Generalized instructions on filing form 4868 for extensions can be found here, and the form 4868 can be found here.  

The Situation

Boston Marathon

At 2:50 pm on Monday, 15 April, two explosions occurred along the Boston Marathon finish line on Boylston Street, about 50 yards apart. At a press conference, Boston Police Department (BPD)’s commissioner reported that the explosions were set off by small, portable explosive devices. The police intentionally detonated another suspicious package by water cannon on Boylston street, but it was later determined that the package was not a bomb. At a 9:45am press conference on 16 April, Governor Deval Patrick confirmed that only two explosive devices were found in the Copley Square area, which were the ones which had detonated, as opposed to speculation that had placed the total number of recovered devices as high as 7 (9:24AM, 16 Apr).

JFK Library

There was also an electrical fire at the John F. Kennedy Library at University of Massachusetts Boston (1:23PM, 15 Apr). BPD currently thinks that it may not have been an explosion (3:38PM, 15 Apr) (2:22PM, 15 Apr), and that it didn't appear to be related to the initial two explosions (6:49PM, 15 April).

MIT Shooting

Witnesses heard gunshots near MIT Building 32 at 10:48PM on Thursday, 18 April 2013. The shooters moved toward Watertown (1:24AM, 19 Apr) (1:07AM, 19 Apr), using a car stolen via an armed robbery (4:21AM, 19 Apr). The car’s owner was found safe (4:21AM, 19 Apr). Later, authorities in MIT reported that the shooter moved off campus, and that it was safe to resume normal, but vigilant activities (2:05AM, 19 Apr).

Watertown Manhunt

Police pursued the suspects to Watertown, facing explosive devices and gunfire. The first suspect was taken into custody and pronounced dead in the hospital (4:20AM, 19 Apr). He was later identified as Tamerlan Tsarnaev. The second suspect was still at large in Watertown (4:20AM, 19 Apr) and later identified as Dzhokar Tsarnaev (8:45AM, 19 Apr).

The following morning, a shelter-in-place notice was put out for “Watertown, Newton, Waltham, Belmont, Cambridge and the Alston Brighton neighborhoods of Boston” (6:18AM, 19 Apr) and later expanded to all of Boston and the surrounding area (9:08AM, 19 Apr). Public transit (6:24AM, 19 Apr) was suspended. Taxi service was briefly suspended (8:16AM, 19 Apr), but later resumed (1:39PM, 19 Apr). Logan Airport remained operational (8:56AM, 19 Apr). Schools across Boston closed for the day (12:39PM, 19 Apr). MEMA also noted that while the shelter-in-place order was in effect, people at work should go home (1:38PM, 19 Apr).

During the search of Watertown, EOD reported that there would be a controlled explosion on Norfolk street on the Cambridge/Somerville line (12:45PM, 19 Apr), later noted as the house of suspected marathon bombers, but no explosives were found and no explosion was needed (5:39PM, 19 Apr). Boston Police asked for websites to stop broadcasting detailed information from police scanners about officer locations (1:14PM, 19 Apr).

At 6:00PM, the suspect remained at large, but the shelter-in-place order was lifted (6:00PM, 19 Apr). The MBTA resumed service, except on commuter rail (6:19PM, 19 Apr).

An hour later, Boston Police announced operations on the Franklin Street in Watertown, asking residents to shelter in place (7:03PM, 19 Apr). Tsarnaev was found hiding inside a boat in a resident’s backyard (9:53PM, 19 Apr) (4:15PM, 20 Apr). Over the next hour and a half, police successfully extracted Tsarnaev alive (8:45PM, 19 Apr)

Casualties

The death toll of the marathon attacks stands at three (7:09PM, 15 Apr) (5:53PM, 15 Apr). Injury reports initially came in at 23 (3:06PM, 15 Apr), but by Tuesday morning, had risen to 176 (9:06AM, 16 Apr) (10:21AM, 16 Apr).

A Brigham and Women’s Hospital spokesperson and a doctor from Mass General Hospital have tentatively concluded that the bombs were filled with anti-personnel shrapnel (9:59AM, 16Apr), including pellets and nails. The doctor also noted that the shrapnel was mostly localized in the lower extremities of the victims. Tufts Medical Center, which treated at least 17 victims, saw a similar localization (16 Apr).

During the MIT attacks and ensuing Watertown manhunt, one MIT officer, later identified as Sean A. Collier (19 Apr), was fatally shot (12:07AM, 19 Apr) (12:13AM, 19 Apr) and an MBTA Police officer was injured (4:21AM, 19 Apr). Suspect Tamerlan Tsarnaev died during the manhunt (4:20AM, 19 Apr). Suspect Dzhokar Tsarnaev is currently alive, but in “serious” condition (5:16PM, 20 Apr).

Police Response

The Boston Police have cordoned off the area around Copley Square for the foreseeable future (4:42PM, 15 Apr).
